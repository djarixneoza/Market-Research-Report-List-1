<p><p>The Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA) is a versatile laboratory technique used to detect and quantify proteins, antibodies, and hormones. The market is categorized into several types: </p><p>1. **Sandwich ELISA**: Utilizes two antibodies to capture and detect the target antigen, ensuring high specificity.</p><p>2. **Indirect ELISA**: Employs a secondary antibody to amplify the detection signal of the primary antibody bound to the antigen.</p><p>3. **Competitive ELISA**: Measures the concentration of an antigen based on competition between a sample and a labeled antigen.</p><p>4. **Others**: Includes variations like multiplex formats and microplate assays, expanding application scope.</p></p>
