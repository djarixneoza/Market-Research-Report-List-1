<p><p>The lubricating improver market is segmented into acidic and non-acidic types. Acidic lubricating improvers are designed to enhance the performance of lubricants by providing anti-wear and corrosion resistance properties, often suitable for high-load applications. Non-acidic lubricating improvers, on the other hand, focus on improving viscosity, oxidation stability, and overall lubricant efficiency without introducing acidity, making them ideal for a broader range of applications. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing lubricant performance and extending equipment life.</p></p>
