<p><p>The photochromic films market is categorized into three types: organic, inorganic, and organic-inorganic hybrid. Organic photochromic films primarily use carbon-based compounds that react to UV light, changing color. Inorganic films utilize metal oxides or other inorganic materials for their photochromic properties, offering durability and thermal stability. Organic-inorganic hybrid films combine both organic and inorganic elements, leveraging the advantages of both types to enhance performance, such as faster response times and improved environmental resistance, making them versatile for various applications.</p></p>
