<p><p>Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO) is a medical procedure that provides cardiac and respiratory support to patients whose heart and lungs are unable to function properly. This advanced life support technology involves the use of a pump to circulate blood through an artificial lung (membrane) that adds oxygen and removes carbon dioxide before returning it to the patient’s bloodstream. ECMO is commonly used in critical care settings for conditions like severe pneumonia, acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS), and cardiac failure.</p><p>The ECMO market is poised for significant growth, driven by rising cases of respiratory and cardiac diseases, technological advancements in ECMO systems, and an increasing number of hospitals adopting these life-support techniques. As healthcare providers focus on improving patient outcomes, there is a heightened demand for advanced therapeutic interventions like ECMO. <p><p>Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO) is a life-support technology used to provide prolonged cardiac and respiratory support. The market is segmented into three types: </p><p>1. **Veno-Arterial (VA) ECMO** supports both heart and lung function by providing oxygenation and circulation through venous and arterial access.</p><p>2. **Arterio-Venous (AV) ECMO** is primarily utilized in dialysis settings, facilitating blood oxygenation while supporting the removal of toxins.</p><p>3. **Veno-Venous (VV) ECMO** focuses on respiratory support, oxygenating blood drawn from veins and returning it to the venous system.</p></p>
