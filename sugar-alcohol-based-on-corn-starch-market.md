<p><p>Sugar alcohols derived from corn starch, including sorbitol, mannitol, erythritol, and maltitol, are widely used in the food industry as low-calorie sweeteners and bulking agents. Sorbitol is commonly used in sugar-free products, while mannitol offers a cooling effect and is often found in confections. Erythritol is favored for its zero-calorie content and minimal digestive side effects. Maltitol, with a taste and texture similar to sugar, is popular in baked goods. Each type serves specific applications based on sweetness, caloric content, and functional properties.</p></p>
